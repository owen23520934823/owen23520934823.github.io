Healthcare Education Should Not Be Restricted

Women in healthcare are increasingly unable to access healthcare in Afghanistan. Specifically, the MSF strongly condemns the restrictions on women to access education and ban on female NGO workers by Afghanistan. Limiting the participation of women in the Afghan workforce will lead to detrimental consequences for all.

About Us

Médecins Sans Frontières (MSF) is an international nonprofit that provides mergency medical humanitarian care to people affected by conflict, epidemics, disasters, or exclusion from healthcare. MSF condemens the measures taken by the Afghanistan government to erase women from social life, prompting the planning of a campaign to address the lack of access women have to healthcare education.

Goals

We work through community-based care and remote training to sustain and protect female healthcare workers.

  • Maintain women’s role in healthcare
  • Ensure access to essential services
  • Operate safely within constraints

The Problem

Following the Taliban's return to power in 2021, severe restrictions on Afghan women's participation in public life and employment — including bans on working for NGOs — have critically limited women's access to healthcare, driving a growing public health crisis particularly in maternal and reproductive health.

Our Mission

Our mission is to preserve and expand safe, culturally-appropriate access to essential healthcare for women and children through community-led services, remote training, and respectful partnerships.

  • Protect and support female healthcare workers
  • Deliver community-based maternal and newborn care
  • Provide remote training and mentorship to sustain skills

We operate with a health-first, non-confrontational approach grounded in local acceptance and safety.
